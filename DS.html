DS

The Nintendo DS was the first member of the Nintendo DS family. It was originally introduced as a side device to nintendo's flagship handheld, the Gameboy Advanced, but with its high demands and flourishing sales, Nintendo decided to make it the successor to the Gameboy Family. It was unique from other consoles with its dual screen display. The two screens worked in sync to provide advanced gameplay capability the Gameboy could not get to. It also featured backwards compatability with Gameboy titles, so it could operate any game the Game boys could. It was simply superior in every way. Since the market demand for handheld consoles was so high, and with a lack of competition at the time, the DS was an instant success and flourished in the market place. Along with its new gimmicks, the DS featured wifi connection. With this, it was able to connect with other DS systems, allowing customers to engage in multiplayer activities. The DS came installed with a camera which allowed for users to take photos of themselves and surroundings. In future years, Nintendo released the DS Lite, a lighter and more efficient DS. This gave players an upgrade to the already beloved DS system. The DS stood strong until 2008 when it was finally suceeded by the Nintendo DSi. To this day the DS is Nintendo's most successful console, and globally, it is second only to Sony's Playstation 2.
