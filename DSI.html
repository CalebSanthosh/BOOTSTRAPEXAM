DSI

In 2008, Nintendo’s DSi was launched. The DSi was intended to succeed the Nintendo DS trims. Nintendo adjusted the proportions of the DSi to be more convenient for the public. The DSi was slimmer and had a slightly larger display. The only sacrifice the DSi had to make was a loss of Gameboy compatibility. The DS had an added slot in the back for Gameboy games, from the earlier Nintendo console to be played on, but the DSi lost it for the sake of being more portable. The DSi also received an upgrade to its hardware and camera systems. This gave it more power and capability especially when running older DS titles. The most significant to the DSi was the addition of a “DSi shop”. This was an online e-commerce program pre-installed into the console which allowed for easy game purchases on the DSi. The DSi did not sell as well as the DS, especially in its first few months, but it was still very successful, nonetheless. It’s only competition at the time was Play station's PS Vita, a portable console developed by Sony. In late 2009, Nintendo launched a Nintendo DSi XL. This rendition of the DSi was much larger than the standard DSi. It sacrificed portability for higher screen resolution and more comfortable usage. This trim was intended for customers who preferred to play it at home where portability was not much of a necessity. The DSi was a significant model in the DS lineup. Although not the most successful, it gave Nintendo the opportunity to experiment with changes and see the reception of the public on behalf of it.
