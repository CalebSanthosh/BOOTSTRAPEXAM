3DS

2011 marked the release of the Nintendo 3DS. The 3DS was the successor and was most known for its 3D capabilities. It had a 3D toggle incorporated into the side of the console. This feature allowed the 3DS to create proper 3D visuals out of the screens. The incorporation of 3D gave the 3DS a far more extensive library of games which could run with beautiful 3D visuals. In addition to the 3D, the 3DS received an upgraded camera and software. Street Pass was another notable feature implemented into the 3DS. It allowed the 3DS to link with other 3DS systems in close proximity. Whenever 2 3DS owners passed by, both 3DS systems would track down the other, and add them to a library of characters. This allowed players of the 3DS to meet new people easier. The 3DS received a revised shop program, known as “Nintendo Eshop”. This system has remained constant in all of Nintendo’s recent consoles, including the Switch. The 3DS had backwards compatibility with all the original DS games, and even had access to some of the Gameboy titles through a program known as “Virtual Console”. Although all the new changes sounded so promising, the 3DS turned out to be a flop. It’s original price of $250 was not appealing to a lot of its prior customers, so in the summer of 2011, Nintendo dropped the price to $170. 20 Eshop titles would be gifted to customers who bought the 3DS earlier in compensation. This price drops significantly boosted 3DS sales and brought it up to be another of Nintendo’s most successful consoles. The 3DS was one of the biggest steps Nintendo took, and it stayed as Nintendo’s flagship model until 2017.
